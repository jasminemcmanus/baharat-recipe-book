about baharat.

An aromatic and peppery Middle Eastern spice blend, baharat means 'spices' in Arabic. It is typically used in the cuisines of the Mashriq region to the east of Egypt, featuring in Lebanese, Jordanian, Syrian, Israeli and Palestinian dishes. Warm and sweet, baharat adds aaromatic depth to soups, tomato sauces, lentils, rice pilafs and couscous. It also makes an excellent rub for fish, chicken and other meats, and can be fried in butter and poured over dal. In Lebanon, it is called seven-spice mix and includes fenugreek. Turkish baharat often includes mint, while the Tunisian version might be perfumed with dried rose petals. In the Persian Gulf, dried lime are sometimes added.
